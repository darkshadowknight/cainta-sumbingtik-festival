Cainta kicks off Sumbingtik Fiesta 2023

Residents of Cainta, Rizal are in a festive mood this week as the town celebrates the annual “Sumbingtik Fiesta.”

“Sumbingtik” is an acronym for suman, bibingka, and latik — three native delicacies traditionally made in Barangay Sto. Niño, Cainta.

The facades of houses in Barangay Sto. Niño are adorned with native materials for the annual November 30 and December 1 fiesta.

Every year, the town government sponsors a house decoration contest where residents decorate their homes using materials like coconut, coconut husks, suman wrappers, and leaves.

Visitors can purchase these delicacies from local stores in Barangay Sto. Niño, and tourists often stop by to take pictures of the decorated houses.

Mayor Elen Nieto's office announced that a grand float parade will take place on Monday, November 27, starting at 4 p.m., with local government employees and officials joining the event.

Traffic rerouting will be implemented as some roads leading to the municipal hall will be closed at certain times.

November 30 marks the feast day of Saint Andrew the Apostle, the town's first patron saint,
while December 1 is observed as the feast day of Our Lady of Light, or "Inang Santisima ng Kaliwanagan."
