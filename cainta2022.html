Cainta kicks off Sumbingtik Fiesta 2023

The SumBingTik Festival is an annual week-long cultural celebration held in Cainta, Rizal, coinciding with the town's founding anniversary and the feast of Our Lady of Light, the town's patron saint. First celebrated in 2014, this year's theme is “Pistang Perya sa Cainta.”

SUMBINGTIK IS BACK AFTER A TWO YEAR HIATUS

After an almost two-year hiatus due to the pandemic, the SumBingTik Festival resumed in Cainta, bringing back a sense of normalcy to the community with a range of festivities catering to all sectors and age groups. This year's theme was “Pistang Perya sa Cainta” (Carnival Feast in Cainta).

A highlight of the festival was the Ms. Gay Queen pageant, showcasing the inclusiveness of the LGBT community. This was followed by the Caindakan Street Dance, a vibrant parade featuring participants from various schools dressed in brightly colored costumes, dancing to the beat of drums, music, and folk songs.

On the second day, Serenata featured an orchestra performance reminiscent of the big bands of yesteryears. The Color Fun Run and Bike Ride promoted health and wellness, while the modern Youth Can Dance competition showcased the best young dancers of Cainta.

LGU Day with Palarong Pinoy provided a much-needed employee engagement activity, boosting the morale of local government employees.

The highlight of the fiesta remained the SumBingTik House Decoration contest. "SumBingTik" is a portmanteau of suman, bibingka, and latik — the town’s signature delicacies made from glutinous rice and coconut. Houses in Barangay Sto. Niño were decorated with these delicacies, and the streets were adorned with large SumBingTik props.

The Grand Float Parade was another highly anticipated event, with 22 floats creatively built from recyclable materials. Each float reflected the theme “Pistang Perya sa Cainta” through depictions of characters, games, and rides.

The festival concluded with a lively Zumba/Aero Marathon held on the feast day of Our Lady of Light.
